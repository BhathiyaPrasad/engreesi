## Usage

### ~ something (with someone)

- I took my friend with me to ask for directions from the police officer.


### ~ something (to someone/something)

- I always take my dog to the vet for regular check-ups.


### ~ someone (to something)

- She took her brother to the store to buy some groceries.


### ~ someone doing something

- She took her daughter sleeping at her roommates' house without asking.


### ~ someone to do something

- She took her daughter to the park.


### ~ someone/something (adv./prep.)

- "We can take the bus to school instead of walking."



### ~ something/someone (from someone/something)

- She took the book from her friend without asking for permission.


### ~ something (as something)

- He always takes the books as they are without asking for any help.


### ~ someone/something (as someone/something)

- He always takes his little sister as his date to the school dance.
